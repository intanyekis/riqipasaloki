As of his career, currently, he is working as a head coach in NCAA Division for South Florida Bulls football. In 1983, he joined the University of Florida coaching staff as a graduate assistant back and later served as a graduate assistant at Texas A&M. In 1986, he did his first full-time coaching job at Southern Illinois.

Later, he joined the South Carolina Gamecocks as a defensive coordinator in 1999. Before the 2003 season, he was hired as defensive coordinator for the Florida Gators. In 2004, he served as interim coach of the Gators for one game. On December 9, 2009, he became the 21st head football coach at the University of Louisville.

In 2011 and 2012, he won two Big East championships with Louisville and led Louisville to a victory in the 2013 Sugar Bowl. On January 6, 2014, he was formally introduced as Texas’ 29th head coach.

He made a career record of 42-30 win-loss as a head coach during the 2014 season but Texas fired him after the 2016 season. He was hired in South Florida one month after leaving Texas. He was able to garner 2 Big East Coach of the Year in 2010 and 2012. As well, he was a 2009 finalist for the Broyles Award.

Reflecting on his personal life, he is a married man who tied the marriage knot with his beautiful wife Victoria. The couple has been blessed with two lovely daughters Hope and Hailee.
