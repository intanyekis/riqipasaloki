Education and Introduction to Golf

Kurt Kitayama attended Chico High School. During the tenure, he was the Senior co-captain and starting point guard for the Chico Blazin’ Heat, his high school’s AAU basketball team. 

Additionally, he has also been named the high-scoring tandem on the court and fellow Chico High alumnus Sterling Smith. Altogether, he also led his basketball team to the 2011 California Northern Section Title.

Moreover, Kitayama also partook in the Championship games when he took his victory over their city-rival Pleasant Valley High School.

Furthermore, later in his high school days, he shifted to golf after realizing he could make it further with it rather than basketball. 

As a junior back in 2007–2008, Kurt even played at the University of Hawaii at Hilo with his brother, Daniel. After certain years of playing likewise, the American-born then started working as a professional caddie. 

Similarly, he also starred at the 2008 U.S. Open course Torrey Pines, South Course, San Diego, California. The very next year, Kurt played in the 2009 Callaway Junior World Golf Championships.

In the meantime, The Junior Golf Association of Northern California titled him as the 2009 Player of the Year. 

In 2009, Kurt graduated from high school and then enrolled in the University of Nevada-Las Vegas. During the tenure, he featured in the top Division I golf program and had flourished by the end of his college days. 

During his four years in UNLV, he bagged an All-American honor and the Academic All-MW team three consecutive years.

As an amateur golfer, Kurt’s highest ranking was the 47th stand, and he won the Jackrabbit Invitational at Primm, Nevada, in 2014 as well as in 2015.

Kurt Kitayama | Professional Career

Kitayama officially turned professional in 2015 and then played for the Web.com tour. During the first few years of the professional days, Kurt had a few successes in the court.

Later in 2018, he mainly played in the Asian Tour. Back then, Kitayama bagged the single Asian Development Tour event, the PGM Darulaman Championship in Malaysia.

During the tour, some of his notable finish was in the New Zealand Open, the Royal Cup, the Asia-Pacific Diamond Cup Golf, and the Japan Golf Tour.

During the same year, he was on the European Tour, where he first claimed victory during the Afrasia Bank Mauritius Open. Besides, he also played in the European Tour Q-School and the 2019 European Tour. 

By the mid-season of 2019, Kurt claimed another European Tour victory by winning the Oman Open. Right then, he was the fastest player to two wins in European Tour history.

Following it, he also played in the PGA Tours. Furthermore, Kurt traveled all around the world for his golf court.
