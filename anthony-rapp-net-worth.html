Early Life and Education

Anthony Rapp, born on October 26, 1971, in Chicago, Illinois, had a passion for acting from a young age. Growing up in Joliet with his mother, older sister, and older brother, Rapp eagerly explored the world of theater through community productions. Attending Joliet West High School, Rapp continued to cultivate his acting skills while balancing academic responsibilities. His dedication earned him recognition and opportunities to showcase his talent. However, Rapp’s ambitions extended beyond high school as he aspired to refine his craft at a higher level.

With dreams of pursuing a career in the film industry, Rapp enrolled in film studies at New York University. Though his passion remained unwavering, he decided to take an unconventional detour by dropping out after one semester, eager to jump into the world of acting headfirst.

This decision proved to be a turning point for the budding actor, setting him on a path of discovery and growth. Rapp’s early life and education laid the foundation for his remarkable journey in the entertainment industry.

Career Beginnings on Stage

In 1981, Anthony Rapp made his stage debut in the off-Broadway show “Youth is Broken.” This marked the beginning of his remarkable journey in the world of theater. Rapp’s talent and dedication quickly caught the attention of audiences and industry professionals alike.

However, it was in 1995 when Rapp achieved a breakthrough that would define his career. He originated the role of Mark Cohen in the off-Broadway production of “Rent.” The musical, written by Jonathan Larson, became a cultural phenomenon and catapulted Rapp into the spotlight.

Not content with just off-Broadway success, Rapp continued to portray Mark Cohen when “Rent” moved to Broadway. He also took the role on a national tour and even performed it on London’s prestigious West End. Rapp’s portrayal of Mark Cohen became iconic, solidifying his status as a talented and versatile stage actor.

Aside from his role in “Rent,” Rapp has graced the stage in various other productions throughout his career. Notable among them are “You’re a Good Man, Charlie Brown” and “If/Then.” These performances showcased Rapp’s range and ability to bring characters to life on the stage.
