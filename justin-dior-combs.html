Justin Dior Combs- Biography

Justin Dior Combs, a 26-year-old Instagram celebrity and rising American Football player, was born on December 30, 1993, under the sign of Capricorn in Mount Vernon, New York State, USA. However, he is best known as the son of Sean Combs – aka Puff Daddy – and costume designer Misa Hylton-Brim.

Justin Dior Combs- Birth, Age, Ethnicity, Siblings, Education

Justin Dior Combs is a Christian who was born to renowned parents. He is American by ethnicity and belongs to a mixed ethnic group. Justin spent his early years in his birthplace of Mount Vernon, where he attended California School before moving on to Catholic Iona Preparatory High School in New Rochelle, New York. He matriculated at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA), where he earned a Bachelor’s degree in Sociology in 2016.

Justin is the eldest biological child of his parents and has five half-siblings. From his father’s on-again, off-again relationship with the late model, actress, and singer Kimberly ‘Kim’ Porter, he has a half-brother named Christian Casey Combs and twin half-sisters D’Lila Star and Jessie James. He has a half-sister, Chance, from his father’s romance with Sarah Chapman, and an adopted brother, Quincy Brown, from Kim Porter and musician Al B. Sure. From his mother’s marriage to Jojo Brim, he has a half-brother named Niko Brim and a half-sister named Madison Brim.

Justin Dior Combs- Relationship, Girlfriend

Between 2010 and 2011, Justin Dior Combs dated actress Paige Hurd. Following their breakup, he began dating model Chantel Jeffries in late 2011, but their relationship ended the following year. Justin Dior Combs- Professional Career

Combs fell in love with American Football at a young age. Justin became a prospective national player while attending Catholic Iona Preparatory High School and joining the school’s squad under coach Victor Quinlo, strengthening his skills and establishing himself as a player. He was ranked 132nd in the cornerback category by ‘ESPN,’ and was named All-State and All-American.

UCLA Scholarship and Disagreement

In 2012, Justin was awarded a football scholarship at UCLA, which sparked outrage in the media. He was chastised for accepting the scholarship because he is the son of a multi-millionaire, despite the fact that the $54,000 scholarship could have helped another student who does not come from a wealthy family.

In his defense, he argued that he received it based on his abilities rather than his family’s riches, which the college agreed with. As a result, Justin joined the UCLA Bruins football team, led by head coach Jim Mora. He was a cornerback until his senior year when he transferred to the position of defensive quarterback. Justin appeared in seven games and had four tackles throughout his college career.

Participation in the Entertainment Industry

Justin Dior Combs also enjoys acting and dancing. In 2008, he made his broadcast debut alongside Quincy Brown in an episode of the MTV reality series “My Super Sweet 16.”

The episode is named after him and is about a spectacular 16th birthday party that took place at a nightclub in New York City. In 2016, Justin made his film debut in Aaron Conaway’s action film “Date With A Hammer.” He has also appeared in other TV shows such as “Hip Hop Squares” (2017), “Wild ‘N Out” (2018), and “Catfish: The TV Show” (2019).
