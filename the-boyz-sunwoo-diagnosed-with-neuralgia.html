THE BOYZ's Sunwoo Struggles With Neuralgia and Neuritis Interrupt Comeback Plans

 Posted on September 8, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  385 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

The management for THE BOYZ has given an update on Sunwoo’s health and his ability to take part in return promotions. On November 24, IST Entertainment stated that Sunwoo had reported discomfort in his neck and shoulders. Initially, the agency had intended to take Sunwoo to the hospital for diagnosis and treatment right away; however, Sunwoo had expressed a strong desire to participate in THE BOYZ’s performance on that day’s episode of “Music Bank,” so they waited until after the pre-recording to take him there.

After Sunwoo went to the hospital, IST Entertainment released a statement that evening saying he had been diagnosed with neuralgia and neuritis in the neck and shoulders.

Doctors have ordered Sunwoo “to minimize excessive movement for the time being,” but he has reportedly shown “strong determination” to participate in THE BOYZ’s return promotions for their new album, which was released this week. Thus, he will carry on “insofar as he can without overexerting himself.”

Why Did The Boyz’s Sunwoo Go on an Indefinite Hiatus in August 2022?

The Boyz’s second Japanese extended play She’s the Boss was released on May 27, 2022; it was the first release on their new label, Universal Music IST. The Boyz’s promotional single “Sweet” was released on Universe on June 17, 2022. The Boyz dropped their seventh EP, Be Aware, on August 16. Sunwoo’s indefinite hiatus from performing was reported on August 30 owing to health concerns.

The Boyz dropped their eighth extended play, titled Be Awake, on February 20, 2023. The R&B Pop dance music “Roar” is the first of six tracks on the EP.

On July 25, the band announced the three-part release of their upcoming second Korean studio album, Phantasy; the first part, titled Christmas in August, was released on August 7. On October 25, the group revealed the second half of Phantasy, Sixth Sense, slated to release on November 20.
