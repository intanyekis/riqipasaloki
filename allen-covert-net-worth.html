Allen Stephen Covert: A Brief Overview

Allen Stephen Covert, born on October 13, 1964, in West Palm Beach, Florida, USA, boasts a prominent career as a comedian, actor, writer, and producer. Recognized for his frequent collaborations with Adam Sandler, his net worth continues to soar. His association with Sandler dates back to their time as classmates at New York University, marking the beginning of a lasting friendship.

According to authoritative sources, Allen Covert’s net worth stood at an impressive $14 million around mid-2016.

Allen Covert’s Career and Achievements

Covert’s debut in the film industry can be traced back to his role as a waiter in Sandler’s first film, “Going Overboard” in 1989. He went on to play significant roles in various Sandler films, such as “The Wedding Singer” (1998), “Little Nicky” (2000), and “50 First Dates” (2004). Despite his presence in non-Sandler films like “Never Been Kissed” (1999) and “Later Last Night” (1999), Covert found a niche in Sandler’s Happy Madison company, where he served as an executive producer and occasional co-writer.

Aside from his acting roles, Covert also made substantial contributions as a writer and soundtrack writer for several Sandler films. His involvement as an executive producer for comedy films such as “Anger Management” (2003), “The Longest Yard” (2005), and “Blended” (2014), further solidified his position in the industry.

In his personal life, Covert is married to Kathryn Covert, and the couple has four children. They currently reside in California, and Covert is known for his support of the Republican Party.
