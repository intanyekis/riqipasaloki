Highlights

  • Bradley Cooper excels in diverse roles, with several high-grossing films, like A Star Is Born and Guardians Of The Galaxy.
  • Cooper's success isn't limited to acting; he's directed films like A Star Is Born and voiced Rocket Raccoon in the MCU.
  • His career includes critical acclaim, box office success, and multiple Academy Award nominations, solidifying his Hollywood standing.

Bradley Cooper is one of Hollywood's most successful and versatile leading men.

Since breaking out in the early 2000s, Cooper has built an impressive career with critical acclaim and massive box office receipts to match.

He has headlined raucous comedies and heartbreaking dramas, voiced a wisecracking space raccoon, and even directed a box office smash.

He has placed four times in the annual rankings of the world's highest-paid actors. Bradley Cooper's net worth is estimated in the vicinity of $120 million.

Cooper has been around for over two decades and has starred in over 40 films, which have collectively grossed over $13 billion at the box office.

He made his film debut in the comedy Wet Hot American Summer, and gained mainstream recognition in the 2005 comedy Wedding Crashers.

His comedic breakout came with the massive success of The Hangover in 2009, which spawned two sequels.

His billion-dollar resume features critically acclaimed hits, including multiple Oscar nominations for his performances in Silver Linings Playbook, American Hustle, and American Sniper, the latter of which he also produced.

By 2014, he began voicing Rocket in the Marvel Cinematic Universe, a role that saw him appear in five other MCU projects.

The Academy Award-winning actor further expanded his artistic horizons by venturing into directing and producing, helming the widely lauded musical romance A Star Is Born in 2018, reportedly earning him his highest single-film paycheck to date.

Cooper directed the biographical drama Maestro, after which he was nominated for the 2024 Oscars — joining a very elite club of actors who have directed themselves into "Best Actor" nominations twice.

10 A Star Is Born (2018)

Global Box Office, A Star Is Born - $436,433,122

Bradley Cooper starred and made his directorial debut in the musical romantic drama A Star Is Born:

He portrayed Jackson Maine, a seasoned musician battling addiction who discovers and falls in love with Ally, a struggling singer played by Lady Gaga.

A Star Is Born received widespread critical acclaim for Cooper, Gaga, and Sam Elliott's performances and Cooper's direction, as well as the screenplay, cinematography, and music.

The film received numerous accolades, including eight nominations at the 2019 Academy Awards. A Star Is Born enjoyed a tremendous box office run, amassing $215.3 million in North America and $220.9 million in other territories.

This brought its global total to $436.2 million, easily exceeding its $36 million production budget, and the film's soundtrack sold over six million copies worldwide.

According to Deadline Hollywood, A Star Is Born made $178.1 million in net profit, when expenses and revenues are factored in.

9 The Hangover (2009)

Global Box Office, The Hangover - $469,328,079

The Hangover launched Bradley Cooper into major stardom as Phil Wenneck, the smooth-talking leader of the infamous Wolfpack.

This outrageously funny comedy follows Phil, Stu, Alan, and their missing friend Doug on a chaotic misadventure through Las Vegas after a bachelor party gone horribly wrong.

According to Box Office Mojo, The Hangover earned $467 million, of which $277 million was from North America and $192 million from other regions, on the back of a $35 million budget.

It was the highest-grossing R-rated comedy ever in the United States, surpassing a record previously held by Beverly Hills Cop for almost 25 years.

8 American Sniper (2014)

Global Box Office, American Sniper - $547,659,020

Bradley Cooper underwent a dramatic physical transformation to portray Chris Kyle, a highly decorated Navy SEAL sniper in this Clint Eastwood-directed war biopic.

Cooper's intense performance earned him an Oscar nomination for "Best Actor." Despite a few technical missteps, Cooper delivers a gripping performance, which led to the film's tremendous box office success.

American Sniper dominated the domestic market in 2014 with $350.1 million, and added an impressive $197 million from other territories.

The figures brought the film's worldwide total to $547.1 million, far surpassing its $58 million production budget.

Deadline Hollywood estimated that the film made a profit of $243 million, making it the second-most profitable film of 2014, only behind Transformers: Age Of Extinction.

7 The Hangover Part II (2011)

Global Box Office, The Hangover Part II - $586,764,305

The Wolfpack reunited for more debauchery in The Hangover Part II, this time jetting off to Thailand for Stu's wedding.

Bradley Cooper reprised his role as Phil, the charismatic ringleader, as the guys found themselves in another blackout situation with hilarious and disastrous consequences.

What was supposed to be a simple pre-wedding brunch spirals out of control, leading to another epic hangover and a desperate retracing of their steps. Building on the success of the first film, the sequel went even bigger.

In its opening weekend, it earned $177.8 million, which was the highest-grossing worldwide opening for a comedy film.

The Hangover Part II ended up grossing $254.5 million in North America and $332.3 million in other territories, for a worldwide gross of $586.8 million, against a budget of $80 million.

6 Thor: Love And Thunder (2022)

Global Box Office, Thor: Love And Thunder - $760,928,081

In Taika Waititi's blockbuster superhero film Thor: Love And Thunder, Bradley Cooper continued his streak of successful voice acting roles, bringing the fan-favorite character Rocket Raccoon to life once again.

This hilarious and action-packed addition to the Marvel Cinematic Universe saw Thor battling a terrifying new threat, Gorr the God Butcher:

Although his screentime as Rocket was quite limited, his comedic chemistry with Chris Hemsworth's Thor remains a highlight of the MCU.

Thor: Love And Thunder received criticism for its screenplay and tonal inconsistencies, while its humor and visual effects were met with polarized responses.

While that was the case, the film continued the Marvel tradition of box office dominance, grossing $343.3 million in the United States and Canada and $417.7 million in other territories, for a worldwide total of $760.9 million.

Love And Thunder earned a net profit of $103 million for Disney Studios, making it the ninth most profitable blockbuster of 2022.

5 Guardians Of The Galaxy (2014)

Global Box Office, Guardians Of The Galaxy - $773,350,147

Guardians Of The Galaxy launched Bradley Cooper's beloved Rocket Raccoon into the Marvel Cinematic Universe.

Praised for its screenplay, direction, acting, humor, soundtrack, visual effects, and action sequences, the film follows Peter Quill and a ragtag group of cosmic misfits, including the wisecracking cybernetically-enhanced Raccoon, as they go on the run after stealing a powerful artifact.

Guardians Of The Galaxy grossed $333.7 million domestically and an estimated $439.6 million overseas, resulting in a worldwide total of $773.3 million.

It emerged as the third-highest-grossing film of 2014 and the highest-grossing superhero film of that year.

Deadline Hollywood calculated the film's net profit at $204.2 million, considering production budgets, marketing expenses, and revenue.

2 Avengers: Infinity War (2018)

Global Box Office, Avengers: Infinity War - $2,052,415,039

Cooper returned to the sound booth again as a member of the Guardians in the epic superhero blockbuster Avengers: Infinity War.

In this colossal Marvel crossover event, the Avengers join forces with the Guardians to battle the intergalactic tyrant Thanos:

Thanos seeks to collect the all-powerful Infinity Stones in a twisted quest to wipe out half of the universe's population.

Boasting a production budget of $400 million, Infinity War ranks among the most expensive films ever made. Infinity War delivered stunning visuals and heart-pounding action sequences.

It received praise for its visual effects, intense action sequences, dark tone, emotional depth, and stirring musical score.

Infinity War conquered the box office by grossing over $2.1 billion worldwide, making it the highest-grossing film of 2018 and the fourth highest-grossing film of all time at the time of its release.

1 Avengers: Endgame (2019)

Global Box Office, Avengers: Endgame - $2,799,439,100

Bradley Cooper's Rocket Raccoon played a pivotal role in the epic conclusion to the Infinity Saga, Avengers: Endgame.

This film saw the surviving Avengers and their allies embark on a daring mission to reverse Thanos' devastating actions.

Endgame delivered an emotionally resonant and action-packed finale to a decade of storytelling.

The film received praise for its direction, action scenes, visual effects, and emotional weight, with critics lauding its culmination of the 22-film story.

Endgame had a worldwide opening of $1.2 billion, the biggest of all time, and surpassed Infinity War's total gross in eleven days.

According to Box Office Mojo, Avengers: Endgame grossed $858.4 million in North America and $1.941 billion abroad, for a worldwide total of $2.799 billion.

It was the highest-grossing film of all time until it was surpassed by Avatar (2009) due to the 2021 re-release in China.

Endgame made a net profit of $890 million, accounting for production budgets and its box office gross.
